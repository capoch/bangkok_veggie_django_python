Dictionary for vegetarians

A basic vocabulary will help you a lot to order Thai food adjusted for vegetarians. I will state here the common phrases to do that – please be aware though that it’s actually not possible to write Thai in our script. You will find websites where you can listen to the sound of the words. The best is to learn Thai in school or with a private teacher, so you can listen how it’s pronounced and they can correct you if you’re doing it wrong.

Basics
To order a dish, you say just “ao/ow” which translates like “take”, followed by the name of the dish. Usually you can put “na ka” (female) / “na krap” (male) in the end of your sentence. “na” helps to emphasize if you ask someone to do something for you, like adjusting your dish vegetarian, and “ka/krap” to be polite.

The important words for you are:
Vegetarian mang-sa-wi-rat
Vegan jay

If you order mang-sa-wi-rat, it will still consist fish sauce etc., just no whole pieces of meat, fish or seafood. If you order jay, there will be no animal by-products at all, but maybe it will be quite tasteless because the strict jay food is without chilli, garlic, onions and some herbs. That’s why I usually order mang-sa-wi-rat and add the information depending on the dish, for example “mai sai nam bplaa” (without fish sauce). But for me it’s easier because I eat egg which is in many dishes. If you eat vegan you will probably be more comfortable to just order “jay”.

Example sentences (Please see the the dictionary below for explanations of the words):
I take vegan Pad Thai please:
“Ao Pad Thai jay na ka/krap”

Do you have vegetarian food here?:
“Tee-nee mee ahan mang-sa-wi-rat mai ka/krap”?

I eat vegetarian:
“Chan (female) / Pom (male) gin mang-sa-wi-rat ka/krap”

I eat vegan:
“Chan (female) / Pom (male) gin jay ka/krap”

I don’t eat meat:
“Chan (female) / Pom (male) mai gin neua-sad ka/krap”

I don’t eat fish:
‘Chan (female) / Pom (male) mai gin bplaa ka/krap’

I don’t eat eggs:
‘Chan (female) / Pom (male) mai gin khai ka/krap”

Please don’t put fish and oyster sauce:
“Mai sai nam bplaa lae nam man-hoy na ka/krap”

Please don’t put shrimp paste:
“Mai sai kapi na ka/krap”

What is inside this dish?:
“Un-nee khue aria ka/krap?”

Usually you can put na ka (female) / na krap (male) in the end of your sentence. “na” helps to emphasize if you ask someone to do something for you and “ka/krap” to be polite.
